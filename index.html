Begin with definitions, because most confusion starts here. Testosterone replacement therapy restores a deficient man's testosterone to a healthy physiologic range using a bioidentical form of the hormone. It is a treatment for diagnosed deficiency, not an enhancement for normal men and not an anabolic cycle. Where the deficiency is real, men commonly report improved energy, mood, sleep, libido, concentration, and lean-mass maintenance. Each of those outcomes is downstream of an accurate diagnosis, which is why this handbook places the diagnostic panel before the treatment, in that order.

The typical path to this subject is a single testosterone reading labeled 'normal,' followed by unresolved symptoms. The chapters here address each step in sequence: the symptoms that justify testing, the full panel that explains a low result, the comparison of delivery methods, the supporting medications for estrogen and fertility, the realistic costs, and the clear contraindications.
